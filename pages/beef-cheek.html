Shredded Beef Cheek

Beef cheek when cooked right will be one of inf not the best thing to touch your dinner table this year.

These smoked then braised beef cheeks will fall apart by the time you take them out of the smoker. They are a super versatile cut of meat. I almost always use mine for tacos, but they also go prefect with mashed potatoes while using the braising liquid as a gravy. Usually the leftovers, if i have any, will go on top of my eggs in the morning. Follow this recipe and feel free to tweak it in any way you like. Don't like coffee, don't add it. Don't like spice, don't add it. Want something sweeter, add brown sugar. But most of all enjoy and don't forget to share.

What you will need

  • First a smoker or an oven will work
  • A cast iron skillet or dutch oven
  • Salt, Pepper, granulated garlic (SPG for short)
  • Beef Stock and coffee for braising
  • Spicy peppers of your choosing
  • Onion

How to Cook Beef Cheek

  1. Depending on your smoker type this step will be may have to be done earlier or can wait, but fire that bad boy up and get it rolling nicely around 250, 220 if you have a small smoker like me.
  2. lets open up the beef cheeks and trim away the fat as need. take out any grilse you can. but don't worry were going to cook these low and slow so most fat will render away.
  3. Pat the the beef cheeks dry, then add you SPG, my go to is a 3:2:1 ratio.
  4. Once the smoker is up to temp add hte beef to the grill grates.
  5. let it roar for about 3 hours or until the cheeks get to about 180 degrees then head to the next step
  6. Saute some thinly sliced onions, add beef stock, coffee, and any spices you would like.
  7. Add the cheeks to the stock, cover and return to the smoker.
  8. once the cheeks hit around 200 degrees, pull em off and shred
